Breaking Bad is a critically acclaimed American television drama created by Vince Gilligan. It aired from 2008 to 2013 and follows the transformation of Walter White (Bryan Cranston), a meek high school chemistry teacher, into a ruthless drug kingpin after he is diagnosed with terminal lung cancer.

Some of the Key themes present in the show are:

  • Morality & Corruption, which basically explains how far a person can go when pushed to the extreme of extremes
  • Power & Ego, which suggests Walter's challenges with his identity and relationships
  • Consequences, which implies that every action Walter makes in the show has escalating repurcussions.
